rgb project

This is another personal project that I created. The colors Red, Black and Green are representative of the pan-african flag. The Pan-African flag — also known as the UNIA flag, Afro-American flag and Black Liberation Flag — is a tri-color flag consisting of three equal horizontal bands of red, black and green. During its month-long convention at Madison Square Garden in New York City, the Universal Negro Improvement Association and African Communities League (UNIA-ACL) formally adopted it on August 13, 1920.

The Universal Negro Catechism, published by the UNIA in 1921, refers to the colors of the flag meaning: “Red is the color of the blood which men must shed for their redemption and liberty; black is the color of the noble and distinguished race to which we belong; green is the color of the luxuriant vegetation of our Motherland.”

These posters were created to represent the struggles that we as black people have gone through and how these colors represent that struggle. The three posters are meant to be hung together. The positions I drew the women in are based on how their posters should be hung.
