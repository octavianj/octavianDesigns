black minds matter ad campaign

As someone that has issues with depression after dealing with it for most of the past decade, this is a project that I felt was necessary in my portfolio. It shows a part of me that was held back from art and design for quite a few years. While there are a few other factors that contribute to the lack of mental health care in the black community (socioeconamic desparities, provider bias, inequality of care, lack of knowledge on the subject, etc) this particular ad focuses on the aspect of it that has affected me the most personally — the stigma against it. As someone that has gone through the problems of dealing with mental health issues, having people tell you that it doesn’t exist does nothing but make the problems worse. I wanted to capture this with this ad campaign that consists of quotes that I’ve heard while dealing with my own problems. Each poster contains a fact that is related to the stigma of people of color and it gives them a call to action to go get help. I chose the blue monotone color scheme because it represents the calmness, peace and understanding that I wished I was able to receive while I was going through those problems.
