octavian designs personal id

After a lot of back and forth, I finally settled on creating an octopus for my logo design. I went through a lot of brainstorming; I came up with a bunch of words that I could describe myself with, and I cross-referenced those words with words I used to define a “graphic designer”. After that, I got to sketching. My old logo was a picture of my face without much thought. I wanted to do something that was more representative of what you get from my work. I also wanted something with vibrant colors.

Other than the obvious connection to my name, I chose the octopus because of its eight legs. The number 8 signifies balance harmony and ambition. It’s also considered karmic, meaning it’s all about giving back. I wanted the octopus because I felt that it could relate that to the different styles I can exhibit with my work. You could also connect it to the various skills I possess and the many hats I can put on within the design field. Being a graphic designer, illustrator, photographer and web designer, I felt that this was a perfect fit. The pencil says, “My main thing is illustration, but I can do the other stuff too!”

With the colors, I attempted to relate them to their African meanings (which is something you’ll see a few times throughout my portfolio). In some parts of Africa, purple means royalty, luxury, wisdom, passion and prosperity. I used this color to signify the quality that clients can expect with my work. I am passionate about design, art, and its impact on people, and if I’m going to exhibit this through my work, I should show it in my logo. Blue means peace, calmness, confidence and affection. I chose the color because it reflects me as a person. I’m confident in my ability to complete the tasks at hand. All in all, I think this logo is the best representation of me and my work.
